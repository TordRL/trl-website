Case study

Field Inspection Canvas App

Role: Lead Power Platform Consultant · Year: 2025 · Stack: Power Apps (Canvas), Dataverse, Power Automate, SharePoint

The client

A facilities management company with ~80 field technicians doing daily safety and equipment inspections across multiple sites. Replace with real client details (or anonymize as "a mid-sized facilities firm" if under NDA).

The problem

Inspections were paper-based. Photos lived on technicians' phones. Reports were typed up back at the office the next day, and anything urgent slipped through the cracks. The back-office team spent roughly 15 hours a week chasing missing forms and re-keying data into SharePoint lists.

The approach

  • Canvas app designed mobile-first, with offline support so field crews could work on sites with poor signal.
  • Dataverse as the data backbone — proper tables for Sites, Assets, Inspections, and Findings, with role-based security.
  • Power Automate flow to route high-severity findings to the duty manager within minutes, with Teams notifications.
  • Power BI dashboard for leadership to see trends across sites.
  • ALM via Solutions + managed pipelines so changes could move Dev → Test → Prod cleanly.
"We stopped losing reports overnight. The Teams notification the day we went live pinged three urgent findings that would have waited until morning." — Operations Manager

The result

  • Inspection cycle time down from 24 hours to under 15 minutes from completion to report.
  • Back-office rework cut from ~15 hours/week to near zero.
  • 100% audit trail on safety-critical findings, with SLA tracking.
  • Client's internal team now owns day-to-day changes — I did a short handover workshop and that was it.

What I'd do differently

Move the Copilot Studio triage agent onto the roadmap from day one — clients always want it six months in, and planning for it up front saves a refactor of the Dataverse schema.
